## Overview

Alcohol-related liver disease (ArLD) and metabolic-associated fatty liver disease (MAFLD) are the two leading causes of chronic liver disease globally. Despite their clinical importance, the molecular mechanisms driving disease progression from steatosis to fibrosis and cirrhosis remain poorly understood.

Our work in this area focuses on identifying and validating early biomarkers of disease progression, with the goal of enabling earlier diagnosis and the identification of new therapeutic targets.

## The FLR Cohort

We co-lead the **Fatty Liver Research (FLR) longitudinal cohort**, a prospective study of over 800 patients with biopsy-confirmed fatty liver disease. This cohort provides a unique resource for biomarker discovery and validation studies.

### Key questions we are addressing:

- What are the earliest molecular signals of hepatic injury in metabolic liver disease?
- Which patients are at highest risk of progression to fibrosis and cirrhosis?
- Can multi-omics data be integrated to build robust prognostic models?

## Alcohol-Associated Hepatitis

We also have a specific focus on alcohol-associated hepatitis (AAH), a severe and often fatal condition with limited treatment options. We use transcriptomic and proteomic approaches to characterise the inflammatory and fibrogenic landscape in AAH liver tissue.
