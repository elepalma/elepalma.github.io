# Overview

Mitochondria are highly dynamic organelles that constantly remodel their structure in response to cellular demands. One of the most intriguing manifestations of this plasticity is the formation of giant mitochondria (megamitochondria) observed in several human liver diseases.

Our group investigates the mechanisms driving megamitochondria formation and their functional consequences for hepatocyte metabolism, survival, and stress responses.

By combining **human liver tissue models with high resolution imaging and morphometric analysis**, we aim to establish how structural mitochondrial adaptations influence liver pathophysiology and whether they represent exploitable biomarkers or therapeutic targets.

This research area connects fundamental mitochondrial biology with clinically relevant liver diseases, including alcohol related liver disease and metabolic dysfunction associated steatotic liver disease.
