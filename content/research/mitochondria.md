## Overview

Mitochondria are central regulators of hepatic metabolism and cell fate. Dysfunction of the mitochondrial network — including impaired oxidative phosphorylation, increased reactive oxygen species production, and dysregulated mitophagy — plays a critical role in the pathogenesis of both metabolic and alcohol-related liver disease.

## Our Approach

We use a combination of **state-of-the-art imaging** (including live-cell confocal and electron microscopy), **proteomics**, and **functional assays** to characterise the mitochondrial phenotype in diseased hepatocytes and in human liver tissue.

### Key research questions:

- How do mitochondrial morphology and function change as fatty liver progresses to fibrosis?
- What are the key mitochondrial protein complexes dysregulated in alcohol-associated hepatitis?
- Can restoration of mitochondrial function be a therapeutic strategy?

## Collaboration with MRC Mitochondrial Biology Unit

This project is conducted in close partnership with the **MRC Mitochondrial Biology Unit** in Cambridge, providing access to expert expertise and specialised equipment for mitochondrial research.

## Multi-omics Integration

We are developing computational pipelines to integrate mitochondrial proteomics data with transcriptomic and metabolomic datasets, aiming to build a systems-level understanding of metabolic reprogramming in liver disease.
