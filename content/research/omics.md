## Overview

The liver is a complex, zonated organ comprising multiple interacting cell types. Understanding how the cellular ecosystem is remodelled during disease requires single-cell and spatial resolution approaches.

## Single-Cell RNA Sequencing

We use **10x Genomics Chromium** single-cell RNA sequencing to profile the transcriptome of thousands of individual cells simultaneously in healthy and diseased liver tissue. This allows us to identify rare cell populations, track cell state transitions, and map intercellular communication networks.

## Spatial Transcriptomics

We apply **Visium** and **Xenium** spatial transcriptomics to preserve tissue architecture while profiling gene expression. This is particularly powerful for studying the spatial organisation of fibrogenic niches — specific microenvironments in which fibrosis-promoting signals are concentrated.

### Key findings to date:

- Identified a novel hepatic stellate cell subpopulation enriched in pericentral fibrogenic niches
- Mapped the spatial distribution of immune-hepatocyte crosstalk in MAFLD
- Built a single-cell atlas of alcohol-associated hepatitis (published in *Nature Communications*)

## Bioinformatics Pipeline

All omics data is processed through our custom bioinformatics pipeline, built on Nextflow and containerised with Docker for reproducibility. Processed data and code are made publicly available via GitHub and Zenodo.
