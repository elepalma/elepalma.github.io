# Overview

The liver is a complex, zonated organ comprising multiple interacting cell types. Understanding how the cellular ecosystem is remodelled A central focus of the lab is the development and application of human organotypic experimental models that preserve the complexity of liver tissue.

In particular, we use and refine precision cut liver slices (PCLS) and tumour slices to study disease mechanisms and test therapeutic strategies in a physiologically relevant environment.

These models maintain the multicellular architecture of the liver and allow us to investigate processes such as:
- mitochondrial dysfunction
- fibrosis and inflammation
- immune cell interactions
- drug responses

By advancing these platforms, we aim to bridge the gap between basic cellular studies and clinical translation, enabling preclinical testing of therapies for steatotic liver disease and liver cancer.
